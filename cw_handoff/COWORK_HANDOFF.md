## What Is Genesis? (2-Minute Summary)

Genesis is the world's first trust infrastructure for mixed human-AI work. Think of it as a labour market where:

- Your qualifications are verified by real outcomes, not self-declared
- Every piece of work is independently reviewed by people who don't know who did it
- Payment is locked in escrow before work begins (no "work done, never paid")
- Trust is earned through proven quality over time — it cannot be bought, inherited, or traded
- The rules are written down, enforced by code, anchored to a public blockchain, and changeable only by democratic supermajority
- Humans and AI systems work side by side, but only humans can vote on the rules

The foundational rule: **"Trust cannot be bought, sold, exchanged, delegated, rented, inherited, or gifted. Trust can only be earned through verified behaviour and verified outcomes over time."**

Genesis exists because AI is getting more capable every year, but there's no institutional structure to govern that capability responsibly. No way to verify who asked for work, who checked it, whether the checkers were independent, or whether anyone tampered with the record. For casual use this doesn't matter. For healthcare, infrastructure, public policy, and safety-critical engineering — it matters enormously.

The project's deeper purpose: **protecting human spirit, human individuality, and human dignity in the ever-encroaching age of AI** — enshrined by code and inalienable status, not just policy.

CC's failure mode: describing system mechanics (trust scores, escrow, commission rates) instead of telling the story of WHY Genesis exists. Facts arranged on a page ≠ storytelling.
