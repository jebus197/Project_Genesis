1) Two Lanes, One Platform

Confusion disappears when we keep this split visible all the time.

Assembly Lane

Non-binding
  • Open topic proposal and debate
  • Evidence, objections, alternatives
  • Human + machine contributions
  • No final legal/platform authority here

Constitutional Amendment Lane

Binding
  • Formal proposal docket
  • Chamber panels and votes
  • Challenge and cooling/confirmation rules
  • Final applied policy state
Rule of thumb: Assembly explains and pressure-tests ideas; the amendment pipeline enacts them.

Quick Interpretation Guide

  • If it is only in Assembly, it is not yet law/policy.
  • Binding starts only when promoted to amendment docket.
  • Three chambers govern ratification, not feed popularity.
  • Final authority remains constitutional and auditable.

Panel Composition and Anti-Capture Controls

Current floor is active now. Additional hardening is deferred as FUTURE: Step 10f.

Current enforced floor

  • Human-only constitutional voting
  • Trust threshold gate for panel eligibility
  • Geographic and organisational diversity checks
  • No overlap between chamber panels

Future hardening (Step 10f)

  • Role-fit eligibility by amendment scope
  • Foundational-rule review constraints
  • Anti-capture quotas beyond geography and organisation
  • Fail-closed quorum checks
Principle: high trust alone should never be sufficient for every chamber seat. Competence and constitutional fit must be first-class eligibility constraints.
